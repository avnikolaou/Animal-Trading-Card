The Gray Wolf

The lion might be the king of animals but you will never find a wolf performing in the circus

  • Scientific Name: Canis Lupus
  • Average Length: 150 centimeters
  • Average Lifes: 20 years
  • Habitat: Forests

The gray wolf or grey wolf (Canis lupus), also known as the timber wolf or western wolf, is a canine native to the wilderness and remote areas of Eurasia and North America. It is the largest extant member of its family, with males averaging 43–45 kg (95–99 lb) and females 36–38.5 kg (79–85 lb).
